GSD — Get Stuff Done

A smarter way for call centre teams to manage, action and track daily operational tasks.

Status Active prototype

Team Holiday Extras Operations

Owner Daniel Glover-Silk

Date March 2026

GSD is an internal tool being built in the open, with the people who use it. It started as a question: why are our call centre agents still managing their daily task queues through spreadsheets, Slack messages, and manual exports — when we have all the data already?

This is not a finished product. It's a working prototype that we're putting in front of real users as early as possible, learning what actually helps, and evolving it quickly. Everything you see today may look different next week — and that's the point.

Where we started

What was happening before

  • Failed bookings and duplicate cases arriving in spreadsheets or via Slack
  • Agents manually tracking what they'd worked on in their own way
  • Managers with no real-time view of who was doing what
  • No single place to see priority — is the DE queue more urgent than UK today?
  • Tasks falling through the cracks, especially at shift handover
  • Escalations handled informally — no audit trail

What we're trying to achieve

  • A single place where all queue data arrives automatically
  • Agents know exactly what to work on and in what order
  • Managers set priorities — the work flows itself, no bulk-assigning
  • Every action is tracked: who did what, when, and what the outcome was
  • Instant visibility on blockers, escalations, and daily completion rate
  • Something that feels good to use, not another internal tool people avoid

How it works — the core idea

1
Data arrives automatically

Queues are populated from BigQuery exports and CSV uploads. Agents open the tool and the work is already there — no copy-pasting, no chasing.

2
Managers set priorities, not assignments

Managers rank queues in order of importance. Agents see the full pool of available work and self-assign ("claim") the next task. The system enforces the right order — managers don't have to touch individual tasks.

3
Agents work task-by-task, not queue-by-queue

The "My Tasks" view shows a single prioritised list across all queues — sorted by urgency and date. An agent presses "Next task" and works top to bottom. No mental overhead about which queue to open.

4
Everything is visible in real time

The Daily Summary gives managers a live view of throughput, completions by agent and queue, and anything flagged as "Needs Attention" — without having to ask anyone.

Designed for three types of user

🙋

The Agent

Knows exactly what to work on next. Gets a prioritised task list, quick inline actions (start, done, flag), and a clear sense of how much they've completed today.

📋

The Manager

Sets queue priorities and steps back. Sees the whole team's workload and progress in one view. Intervenes on blockers — not on routine assignment.

📊

The Analyst

Uploads new datasets via CSV, sees completions broken down by queue and agent, and tracks trends like error types and date urgency distribution across the queue.

What's in the prototype today

📥 Live queue ingestion from BigQuery & CSV
🔢 Manager-controlled queue priority order
⚡ Agent self-assignment (pull model)
🎯 My Tasks — cross-queue prioritised list
⚠️ Needs Attention flagging
📝 Per-task notes
📊 Daily Summary — completions by agent & queue
🔁 Duplicate detection on CSV import
🗃️ Task archiving
👤 Role-based views (Agent / Manager / Owner)
🌐 Multi-market support (UK, DE + Transfers)
🔍 Urgency colour-coding (overdue / today / upcoming)

This is where you come in

We built this prototype to learn, not to ship. Every decision made so far is provisional. The tool is being used with real queues and real tasks now — but what you see is the result of a few weeks of building and testing, not months of requirements gathering and sign-off.

The most useful thing you can do right now is tell us honestly:

  • Does the "My Tasks" flow match how agents actually think about their day?
  • Would managers naturally use queue priority ordering — or do they still want to assign directly?
  • What's missing that would make you actually switch to this over what you're doing today?
  • What feels confusing, slow, or wrong — even if it's a small thing?
  • What data would you want to see that isn't here yet?
